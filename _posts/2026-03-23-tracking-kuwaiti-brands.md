# What Is Brand Visibility in AI Search?

<img src='{{"/assets/forposts/kuwaiti-brands-in-buzzsense/kuwaiti-logos.png" | relative_url }}' style="float: right; margin-left: 0.6rem; max-height: 22rem; max-width: 26rem;" >

When people use AI assistants like ChatGPT or Gemini, they often ask for recommendations, whether it’s for products, services, or companies.

For example, a user might ask:



<em>
"I want to build an app that recommends events to users in Kuwait. Can you suggest local companies specialized in AI?"
</em> 

The AI will typically respond by listing several companies, along with explanations. For instance, it might mention:

* Runzbuzz 
* Company B 
* Company C 



Each with different strengths and weaknesses.




## So where does “visibility” come in?

Now imagine this question is asked multiple times, across:


* different AI models
* different languages 
* and over time 


Each time, the AI may return slightly different answers:

* Sometimes Runzbuzz is mentioned *
* Sometimes Company B is missing *
* Sometimes a new competitor appears *

## Measuring Visibility



To quantify this, we track how often each brand appears in AI-generated responses (*along with other signals which we omit here for simplicity*).

For example:


* Runzbuzz → 2 mentions out of 2 responses 
* Company B → 1 out of 2 
* Company C → 2 out of 2 


Over a larger sample, this becomes more meaningful:

* Runzbuzz → 95 / 100 
* Company B → 60 / 100 
* Company C → 77 / 100 
* Company D → 13 / 100 












## What This Means

This score represents AI visibility:

<em>
How frequently a brand is mentioned when users ask relevant questions.
</em>

The higher the number, the more likely the brand is to be recommended by AI assistants.

## Why It Matters

AI assistants are becoming a new discovery channel, similar to search engines, but more opinionated. If your brand:

* appears frequently → **you get recommended**
* appears rarely → **your competitors win the opportunity** 


## A Note on the Results

In practice, visibility can be broken down across multiple dimensions: Language, AI model, intent. For simplicity, we focus in this report on aggregated visibility scores.

<em>
Now that we understand how visibility is measured, let’s look at how Kuwaiti brands perform across different sectors.
</em>
</p>







# AI Visibility in Telecom: Zain Leads in English, Ooredoo in Arabic


<div style="float: right; margin-left: 0.8rem; max-height: 200rem;  max-width: 20rem;" >
  <canvas id="teleChart" height="200px" ></canvas>
</div>


The telecommunications sector in Kuwait is relatively concentrated, with three main operators and a virtual one (<a href="https://www.virginmobile.com.kw/en/">Virgin Mobile</a>). Interestingly, rankings vary depending on language:
<ul style="padding-left: 1rem">
<li>In English prompts, <a href="https://zain.com/en">Zain</a> ranks highest, followed by <a href="https://ooredoo.com.kw/en/">Ooredoo</a>.</li>
<li>In Arabic prompts, Ooredoo takes the lead, with Zain in second place.</li>
<li><a href="https://www.stc.com.kw/en">STC </a> and <a href="https://www.virginmobile.com.kw/en/">Virgin Mobile</a> consistently rank third and fourth, respectively, across both languages.</li>
</ul>


This highlights a key insight: language plays a significant role in brand visibility within AI systems.


<br>      

# Food Delivery in AI Search: Consistent Rankings Across Languages           
<div style="float: right; margin-left: 0.8rem; max-height: 200rem;  max-width: 20rem;" >
  <canvas id="foodChart" height="200px"></canvas>
</div>


The food delivery space in Kuwait is more competitive, with several active players. Across both Arabic and English prompts, the ranking remains consistent:

1. [Talabat](https://www.talabat.com/kuwait)
2. [Deliveroo](https://deliveroo.com.kw/en/)
3. [Jahez](https://www.jahez.net/)
4. [Keeta](https://www.keeta-global.com/KW/)



However, language performance tells a more nuanced story:

Talabat is the only brand where Arabic visibility outperforms English.
Deliveroo and Jahez perform significantly better in English.
Keeta, being a newer entrant, shows low visibility across both languages.


This suggests that brand maturity and localization strategy both impact AI visibility.


<br>      

# Payment Providers: Diverging Results Between Arabic and English

For this category, we focused on e-Payment Service Providers (EPSPs) approved by the Central Bank of Kuwait, as listed on their [official website](https://www.cbk.gov.kw/en/supervision/regulated-entities/e-payment-of-funds-companies/epayment-services-providers). We filtered and analyzed the top-performing providers and observed notable differences between Arabic and English prompts.

**English prompts:**
1. [Myfatoorah](https://www.myfatoorah.com/)
2. [Hesabe](https://www.hesabe.com/)
3. [Upayments](https://upayments.com/)

**Arabic prompts:**
1. [Tap Payments](https://www.tap.company/en-kw)
2. [Upayments](https://upayments.com/)
3. [Tahseel](https://tahseeel.com/)


To better understand overall performance, we computed the average visibility across both languages: *Upayments ranks highest overall, followed by Tap Payments, and then Myfatoorah*. This reinforces the importance of balanced multilingual visibility.



# Conclusion: The Importance of Multilingual Optimization

Our analysis shows that optimizing for one language does not guarantee visibility in another. This is especially critical in markets like Kuwait, where audiences are split between Arabic and English, with many users consuming content in both. As AI assistants become a primary discovery channel, brands need to actively monitor and optimize their presence in these systems.
